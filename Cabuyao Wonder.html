Cabuyao, situated in Laguna, Philippines, is a dynamic municipality known for its industrial growth and strategic location near Metro Manila. Home to a thriving economy driven by manufacturing and commerce, it offers diverse employment opportunities and modern amenities amidst its rich cultural heritage. As Cabuyao continues to evolve and embrace modernity, it remains firmly rooted in its cultural traditions and historical legacy. With its blend of industrial progress, culinary delights, and cultural vibrancy, Cabuyao offers visitors a unique and enriching experience that embodies the essence of Filipino hospitality and heritage.

St. Polycarp Parish Church

Locaion: Poblacion Uno, Cabuyao, Laguna

Contact: (049) 531 2168

Google Reviews: 4.7/5.0 (163 Reviews)

A living reminder of the rich fabric of Catholic and Philippine history, St. Polycarp Parish in Cabuyao, Laguna, is more than just a house of worship. Founded centuries ago during the Spanish colonization era, the parish has endured the test of time, witnessing the victories and hardships experienced by the Filipino people. Catholicism deeply ingrained Filipino society during the Spanish era, with establishments like St. Polycarp Parish serving as strongholds of the faith and hubs of communal activity. The parish persevered during times of unrest and resistance, giving the faithful comfort and fortitude amid the chaos of colonization.

Despite Polycarp Parish remaining steadfast in its adherence to Catholic beliefs and principles, it continued to change to meet the needs of its growing membership as the Philippines entered the modern era. The parish's history embodies the enduring legacy of faith and fortitude that defines the country's cultural identity, reflecting the Filipino spirit's tenacity and perseverance. St. Polycarp Parish is a living example of the Filipino people's unwavering faith, a source of inspiration and hope for future generations. Its historical significance serves as a reminder of the Philippines' long Catholic past and the critical role that churches have played in shaping the country's history and culture.

In conclusion, the parish acts as a hub for social and cultural events in Cabuyao. The celebration of common traditions and practices helps the parish preserve the rich cultural heritage of the Filipino people for future generations. Festivals, get-togethers, and community events unite people and build a sense of belonging among inhabitants.

Marcos Twin Mansion

Location: Brgy. Casile, Cabuyao, Laguna

Opened: 1987

Google Reviews: 4.4/5.0 (830 Reviews)

Constructed in the 1970s, the Twin Mansions were envisioned as luxurious retreats for the Marcos family, showcasing their immense wealth and influence. Situated on sprawling estates, these palatial residences were designed to exude grandeur and extravagance, featuring lavish amenities and architectural flourishes that befitted the stature of the First Family.

The Twin Mansions are something to behold, with a unique architectural style that blends modern and traditional Filipino design. From their grand facades to their meticulously cared-for gardens, these buildings are a tribute to the skill and creativity of the architects and craftsmen who built them. Every little detail, from the fancy balconies to the intricate woodwork, reflects the wealth and elegance of their owners. But behind their beauty lies a shadow of controversy. The Twin Mansions serve as a reminder of the dark days of the Marcos regime. During martial law, these luxurious homes stood as symbols of the Marcos family's power and corruption, starkly contrasting with the poverty and suffering experienced by many Filipinos at the time.

Today, the Twin Mansions hold a unique place in Philippine history, serving as a tangible link to a bygone era of political turmoil and social upheaval. As relics of the Marcos era, they offer valuable insights into the complexities of power and privilege in Philippine society, sparking discussions about accountability, justice, and national identity.In the heart of Cabuyao, Laguna, the Twin Mansions stand as silent witnesses to a chapter in Philippine history that continues to shape the nation's identity and trajectory. As historical artifacts, they remind us of the complexities of power, privilege, and responsibility, urging us to strive for a more just and equitable society for all Filipinos.

Puto Pao

San's Bakeshop (Home of the Original Puto Pao)

Location: Brgy.Banay-banay, Cabuyao, Laguna

Google Reviews:

Cabuyao is one of the cities that offers some of the unique cuisines in Laguna, unbeknownst to their rich heritage, their culinary boasts a variety of dishes that can showcase the creativity and flavors of the Filipino cuisine.

Puto pao is one of those examples, it is a Filipino steamed bun that combines the elements of the two of the most popular Filipino dishes, puto and siopao. Puto, a steamed rice cake, and Siopao, a steamed bun filled with savory meat. This dish has an exterior that resembles a puto, being soft, fluffy, and slightly sticky, while inside has a filling similar to siopao, often made with savory ingredients and hard-boiled eggs. This modern dish of two of the most popular Filipino treats, is a testament to the innovative minds of Filipino cuisine, offering a delightful blend of sweet and savory flavors in every bite.

Tasting this dish for the first time is a one of a kind experience and definitely one of the core memories you can take in visiting Cabuyao. Puto Pao has been spread out along with the surrounding cities, however most of the tourists and residents alike, typically prefers the Puto Pao in the San’s Bakeshop in Katapatan than the bakeries on the surrounding cities, because of the freshness and fluffiness of our puto pao. It really is a must try delicacy here in Cabuyao, especially for the tourists.

Kapeng Barako

Cafe de Cabuyao

Location: Brgy.Casile, Cabuyao, Laguna

Google Reviews: 4.8/5 (19 Reviews)

Kapeng Barako is a type of Coffee liberica that has been cultivated in the Philippines for hundreds of years. It has a full-bodied flavor and an intense, somewhat woody aroma that deviates from other coffee types. This type of coffee generation started previously only in the provinces of Batangas and Cavite but nowadays, it has become a way to define Filipino society. The drink’s name Barako in Filipino refers to a person or a stud, which means that the drink has a strong and robust approach to the person that ingests it.

Café de Cabuyao is considered a treasure for those who are truly fond of coffee and want to taste the whole range of Kapeng Barako and the flavor and also enjoy the view in Brgy. Casile. The café offers a variety of ways to enjoy this Filipino classic. From traditional brewing methods to modern twists, such as cold brews and flavored variations, there is something to suit every palate.

One of the key aspects that sets Café de Cabuyao apart is its commitment to supporting local coffee farmers. By sourcing Kapeng Barako directly from farmers in Cabuyao, the café ensures that its coffee is of the highest quality while also contributing to the sustainability of local agriculture. This practice not only helps preserve the tradition of Kapeng Barako cultivation but also supports the livelihoods of Filipino farmers. It is a must visit for coffee lovers and travelers alike, it is truly an astonishing and one of a kind experience.

Batingaw Festival

Date: January 16

The Batingaw Festival, one of the joyful celebrations with deep tradition and folklore in the heart of the municipality of Cabuyao, Philippines. The celebration lasts for five days and is a yearly celebration of the famous Kampanang Ginto in which the said place is considered a city rich of wealth and success. Running for three days there were a number of events scheduled throughout the Batingaw festival such as street dancing and cultural exhibitions, all of which symbolizes the heart and soul of this vibrant town with a rich history.

Cabuyao, Philippines hosts the colorful Batingaw Festival, which draws its inspiration from the myth of the mysterious Kampanang Ginto, a bell believed to bring wealth. The celebration begins with the ringing of church bells, symbolizing abundance for all attendees. The golden bell serves as the focal point of the Kampanang Ginto Parade, which showcases inventiveness and tradition. Exhibitions and contests celebrate local skill and craftsmanship, while colorful street dance and cultural presentations highlight Cabuyao's distinctive background. A stunning firework show marks the end of a week of festivities and leaves a lasting impression on all those who attend the festival.

In conclusion, the Batingaw Festival exemplifies Cabuyao's rich cultural legacy and thriving sense of community. The festival fosters a sense of unity and pride in their shared identity by uniting people through its colorful events and sincere celebrations. The Batingaw Festival serves as a reminder of the timeless values that unite the people of Cabuyao while also serving as a beacon of hope and prosperity for future generations, inspired by the legend of the Kampanang Ginto.

A time of joy, community, and festivities, Christmas is observed in Cabuyao, an exciting city in the Philippines. The streets and houses become decorated out in vibrant lights and decorations as December draws near, resulting in a charming ambiance that uplifts the spirits of the locals. The Cabuyenos eagerly await this time of year since it is not only a religious festival but also a cultural occasion that deepens ties between family and friends.

These rides provide endless entertainment and become a central hub for families and friends to gather and enjoy the festive season. Alongside the rides, food stalls offer an array of local and festive delicacies, from traditional Filipino dishes like lechon and bibingka to popular street foods such as kwek-kwek and fish balls. The combination of thrilling rides and mouth-watering food creates a lively and joyful environment, embodying the spirit of Christmas.

In conclusion, the Christmas celebration in Cabuyao is a beautiful blend of tradition, community, and festivity. The presence of rides and an abundance of delicious food makes it a unique and memorable experience for the Cabuyenos. This festive period not only highlights the rich cultural heritage of the city but also fosters a sense of unity and happiness among its residents, ensuring that the Christmas spirit remains alive and vibrant in the hearts of everyone in Cabuyao.

Santo Nino de Cabuyao Festival

Date: April 8

Santo Niño, or the feast of the Child Jesus, is celebrated on the third Sunday of January, which is especially meaningful to the community. The vibrant Santo Niño de Cabuyao Festival follows a mass in the Cabuyao Church and is a deeply ingrained annual event that shapes the community's culture and customs. The festival, which began in 1981 as an act of thanks from the Alimagno family, has developed into a beloved custom that unites the residents of Cabuyao in joyful adoration.

The celebration of Santo Niño in Cabuyao has its origins in the late 1970s, when the Alimagno family organized a large-scale event in honor of Santo Niño, bringing the town together in prayer and joy after their son Kennedy survived a near-fatal accident. The Santo Niño de Cabuyao Festival is the focal point of this; it is a colorful parade that showcases the locals' intense devotion and faith through song, dance, and pictures of Santo Niño. Communities and families work together to be ready for the occasion, reinforcing their commitment with group prayers and offerings. This festival has expanded over time, bringing in new followers and cementing the ties between the locals and the faith in Cabuyao.

To sum up, Cabuyao's Santo Niño celebration is more than just a festival; it is evidence of the city's enduring faith and rich legacy. This yearly festival, known as the Santo Niño de Cabuyao Festival, is a demonstration of communal devotion and celebration, having humble origins as an act of thankfulness by the Alimagno family. Every year, the residents of Cabuyao get together to celebrate Santo Niño, which serves to both reinforce their faith and fortify the ties that bind them together as a community.

City Fiesta

Location: Cabuyao, Laguna, Philippines

Cabuyao, a thriving city in Laguna, Philippines, is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant community spirit, exemplified by its annual City Fiesta. Held every February, this grand celebration unites Cabuyeños in honoring their patron saints, showcasing local traditions, and celebrating the unity and joy that define their city. The fiesta is both a religious observance and a testament to the enduring spirit of community and cultural pride in Cabuyao.

The City Fiesta features a wide range of events reflecting the city's diverse culture. It begins with a solemn Mass and a grand procession honoring Our Lady of the Holy Rosary and Saint Polycarp. These ceremonies instill a sense of reverence and gratitude among participants. Following the religious observances, the city bursts into a lively celebration with parades, street dancing, and music, showcasing local talents. Traditional games, food fairs, and cultural exhibits further enrich the fiesta, offering a delightful mix of old and new that appeals to both residents and visitors.

More than just an annual event, Cabuyao's City Fiesta celebrates the city's identity, heritage, and community spirit. Through its blend of religious devotion, cultural displays, and communal activities, the fiesta reinforces community bonds and highlights Cabuyao's rich traditions. As Cabuyeños gather each year, they honor their past and patron saints while reaffirming their commitment to the values of unity, joy, and cultural pride that define Cabuyao.
